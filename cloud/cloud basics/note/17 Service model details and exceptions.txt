17 Service model details and exceptions

Service models
1. Platform as a Service (PaaS)
2. Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS)
3. Software as a Service (SaaS)


Additional Service models
1. Function as a Service (FaaS)
	> cloud provider provides a way for user to execute their defined functions
	> ex: AWS Lambda
2. Container as a Service (CaaS)
	> cloud service model that allows users to upload, edit, start, stop, rate and otherwise manage containers, applications and collections



Parts of Cloud that are managed by Cloud Provider for different Service Models (from high to low level)
 > SOURCE: https://azure.microsoft.com/en-us/resources/cloud-computing-dictionary/what-is-iaas/#overview

1. Hosted applications
2. Development tools, database management, business analytics
3. Operating systems
4. Servers and storage
5. Networking firewalls/security
6. Data center physical plant/building




Cloud Provider responsibility for each Service Model
1. SaaS
	> everything is their responsibility (1-6)
2. PaaS
	> all but no.1 is their responsibility
3. IaaS
	> all but no 1-3 is their responsibility


NOTE:
1. Regardless of the Service model you use, you must remember that in the end, what matters is that 
	a. You want control over what matters to you
	b. You want the provider to be responsible for the rest
